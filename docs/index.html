Mirror3D: Depth Refinement for Mirror Surfaces

Jiaqi Tan

Weijie Lin

Angel X. Chang

Manolis Savva

Simon Fraser University

We present the task of 3D mirror plane prediction and annotate several popular RGBD datasets Matterport3D, ScanNet, NYUv2 with 3D mirror planes to establish benchmarks for RGB and RGBD-based 3D mirror plane prediction and depth estimation. Our benchmarks show that both the existing RGBD dataset `ground truth' raw depth data, and the output of state-of-the-art depth estimation and depth completion methods exhibit dramatic errors on mirror surfaces. We propose a simple architecture for 3D mirror plane estimation that can refine depth estimates and produce more reliable reconstructions compare left and right depth and point cloud pairs from NYUv2 dataset.

Abstract

Despite significant progress in depth sensing and 3D reconstruction, mirror surfaces are a significant source of errors. To address this problem, we create the Mirror3D dataset: a large-scale 3D mirror plane annotation dataset based on three popular RGBD datasets Matterpot3D, NYUv2 and ScanNet containing 4,852 mirror instance masks and 3D planes. Armed with this data, we develop a simple Mirror3DNet module that can refine raw sensor depth or estimated depth output to correct errors on mirror surfaces. The main idea of our architecture is to try to estimate the 3D mirror plane based on RGB input and surrounding depth context, and use this estimate to directly regress mirror surface depth. Our experiments show that the Mirror3DNet module can significantly mitigate mirror depth errors from a variety of input depth data, including raw sensor depth and state-of-the-art depth estimation or completion methods.
